The Mandalorian
Directed by Jon Favreau

Time Period: 9 ABY

The Mandalorian takes place 5 years after the fall of the Galactic Empire and follows fellow bounty hunter, Din Djarin, who completes a variety of quests across the galaxy. The main premise of the show focuses on Din following one bounty that has him retrieve a child known as Grogu. After interacting with Grogu, Din goes against the bounty and decides to protect the child from other hunters and fighters of the remaining Imperial army.

This TV series is the most popular exclusive that the Disney+ streaming service has and is the very first live-action Star Wars television adaptation. Throughout this series, there are a lot of thematic elements that are reminiscent of that of the class Western, with the Mandolarion being the rough and tough cowboy. In this unique story, we run into a variety of characters that are new to audiences and some that audiences have seen in previous media.

The Book of Boba Fett
Directed by Various Creators

Time Period: 9 ABY

The Book of Boba Fett takes place right after the events of The Mandalorian Season 2 and shows us where Boba Fett has been for the past 5 years after falling into the Sarlac Pit back in Return of the Jedi. Throughout this show, we learn that Fett had established a bond with the Tusken Raiders and had even joined their tribe. Now after returning to Tatooine, Boba Fett takes the throne of former leader Jabba the Hutt and works with Fennec Shard to rule the criminal underground not with fear, but respect.

Similar to the style of The Mandalorian, the Book of Boba Fett uses a variety of well-known directors and writers to continue the story of the infamous bounty hunter. This show revisits the main character, by having a variety of flashbacks to his life as a Tusken Raider, while also highlighting his new role as the big boss of Tatooine.

Star Wars: Episode VII The Force Awakens
Directed by J.J. Abrams

Time Period: 34 ABY

The Force Awakens is the first installment of the newly established sequel trilogy that takes place 30 years after the fall of the Empire in Return of the Jedi. In this story, we follow both, Rey, habitant of Jakku, and Resistance's fighters, Finn and Poe Dameron, as they work together to complete a mission that could turn the tide of the galactic war. This mission is to find the lost Jedi Master, Luke Skywalker.

This film starts the new trilogy by hitting the refresh button by introducing fans to new characters. We also see that this film uses classic Star Wars tropes to help keep the die-hard fans interested, while also bringing back characters such as Han Solo and Leia Organa to establish the connection to previous trilogies. Episode VII can tackle themes that deal with standing up for the greater good and what it means to be one with the Force.

Star Wars: Episode VIII The Last Jedi
Directed by Rian Johnson

Time Period: 34 ABY

The Last Jedi is the second film within the newly established sequel trilogy of the Skywalker saga and takes place right after the events of The Force Awakens. This film focuses on telling two stories at once, one focusing on the Resistance fighting against the attacks of the First Order and the other following Rey as she is trying to learn the ways of the Jedi from former Jedi Master Luke Skywalker.

This film, similar to the last installment, in providing a lot of nostalgia for fans both new and old, while also providing a brand new Star Wars story to stand out from the others. Episode VIII is recognized as the weakest of the new trilogy due to confusing plot holes but is still capable of providing fans with that classic Star Wars formula.

Star Wars: Episode IX The Rise of Skywalker
Directed by J.J. Abrams

Time Period: 35 ABY

The Rise of Skywalker is the epic conclusion to the Skywalker Saga and also helps to wrap up the new trilogy that was introduced back in 2015. This film continues a year after the previous episode, The Last Jedi, and shows the Resistance continuing their plans to overthrow Supreme Leader Kylo Ren and bring order back to the galaxy. Within this movie, Emperor Palpatine is also somehow back and the Resistance will also have to face an overwhelming Imperial armada.

In this film, we see a variety of new and old characters from past iterations of the Skywalker Saga work together for the greater good. This film is filled with action and plot twists to tell a story of understanding oneself and never giving up, even when the odds are against you.
