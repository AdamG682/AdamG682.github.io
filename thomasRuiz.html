Republic Era (1000-19BBY)

Star Wars: Episode 1- The Phantom Menace Directed by George Lucas

Time Period: 32 BBY

The Phantom Menace was the first movie to release after the initial trilogy had released. Coming out in 1999, This was a very ambitious project for George Lucas to take on due to the scale of it. The Phantom Menace was the first movie to have full 100% CGI shot. The story follows Jedi Knights Qui-Gon Jinn and Obi-Wan Kenobi who are tasked at stopping an invasion of droids on Naboo. Later on they travel to the planet of Naboo where they come in contact with a young boy named Anakin Skywalker.

Filled with some of the best choreography and visuals at the time, The Phantom Menace was met with very mixed reviews, most negative reviews pointed at the very controversial Jar Jar Binks. Jar Jar, a gungan became a notorious character in the star Wars community, some saying he's a good comic relief and others saying he was an annoyance. We were also introduced to the one of the greatest Star Wars scores, The Duel of Fates.

Star Wars: Episode II- Attack of the Clones Directed by George Lucas

Time Period: 22 BBY

Releasing in 2002, Attack of the Clones was the second movie in Goerge Lucas' new trilogy. Starting off strong, the Sith were ordered to kill Queen Amidala but failed and isnntead killed a body double. Obi Wan is on his own journey and is trying to investigate to clones and see who ordered the assasination of Amidala.

Upon Kenobi's investigation he stumbles upon the planet Kamino where the clone troopers are being manufactured for the Republic alliance. Here he meets with Jango Fett who is the blueprint for all clones and believes him to be the hired killer. The Jedi council is made aware of Count Dooku's plans to create a droid army and go after him to stop the process. Dooku tries to make an escape injuring Kenobi and Anakin in the process, but is met by Yoda in his final stretch.

Star Wars: The Clone Wars Directed by Dave Filoni

Time Period: 22 BBY

Star Wars: Clone Wars was a TV showed that premiered on the channel Disney XD. The story took place in-between Episode II and III and follows jedi Knights Obi-Wan Kenobi, Anakin Skywalker, and his padiwan Ahsoka Tano. We follow the adventures of these 3 as they try and stop Emperial invasions of Droids on Rebel planets. Along the way we see Anakin's struggle when balancing the ligh and dark side of the force that is continued through Episode III- Revenge of the Sith

The Clone Wars was seen as a revolutionary animation because at that point, not many shows were given a cinematic budget on their level. The initial series stopped airing after 6 seasons from 2008-2014 until the 7th seasons aired on Disney+ in 2020. The show became so popular that the characters Bo Katan and Ahsoka Tano were written into the live action shows Mandalorian and The Book of Boba Fett.

Star Wars: Episode III- Revenge of the Sith Directed by George Lucas

Time Period: 19 BBY

The best reviewed prequel movie, Star Wars: Revenge of the Sith brought us a true drama filled with hate, sadness, and hope. here we see the final battle Anakin Skywalker must face against both the sith and Jedi.

We first see Chancelor Palpatine in the hands of Count Dooku who is soon rescued and returned to the Jedi council. On the way Obi Wan is met with General Grievous who he fights. While this is happening Anakin is having a very deep conversation with Palpatine on the powers the dark side could hold and this is the final weight put on Anakin to tip the scales of the force. Palpatine reveals himself as a sith, orders the killing of all Jedi through the clone army, and Anakin feels the true power that anger holds.

Star Wars: The Bad Batch Directed by Dave Filoni

Time Period: 19 BBY

The Bad Batch takes place right after the events of Order 66, in Episode III. Throughout this series, we are witness to the elite group of clone troopers known as Clone Force 99, a platoon that is best known for its uniqueness to other clones. This unit of troopers fight through daring missions as they have disobeyed the direct orders to overthrow the Galactic Republic.

I feel that this show is the perfect follow-up to the event of Star Wars: The Clone Wars and Episode III as it puts us in the eyes of clone troopers who were able to disobey direct orders and separate themselves from the other mindless clones who followed. I also appreciate how this show can demonstrate how each character has rightfully earned their spot on the team as they all possess unique skills. I also find that the animation can superceed what was done in the Clone Wars and make it even more visually appealing.

Solo: A Star Wars Story Directed by Ron Howard

Time Period: 13 BBY

Have you wondered how Han Solo met Chewbacca? Or the history between Han Solo and Lando Calrissian and how he won the Millennium Falcon in a card game? Well Solo: A Star Wars Story can answer all of those questions and more by taking a young Han Solo through an adventure of betrayal and his witty intuition. In this film, you'll see Han Solo perform a variety of feats such as partaking in a heist with a smuggler crew and fighting against the crime syndicate known as the Crimson Dawn.

This Star Wars story can separate itself from others as it can shine more of a light on the background of the scoundrel known as Han Solo. The film is viewed as one of the weaker installments in the Star Wars cinematic universe, as it primarily thrives on nostalgic feelings and answering questions that make Han Solo who he is. This included the iconic card game with Lando that won him the Millennium Falcon and also how Han met Chewie. I feel that this movie would've been better received if it has tackled a more well-known Han Solo story or even taken place after the events of Return of the Jedi.

Star Wars: Rebels Directed by Dave Filoni

Time Period: 5 BBY

Star Wars: Rebels takes place 14 years after Order 66, a command in which we saw clones turn against the Jedi Order, and the Republic fell and turned into the Empire. This show introduces us to a new set of characters that have been able to survive against the rise of Imperial forces and focus their eyes on weakening the Empire through a variety of covert operations. This group is widely diverse from having a Jedi Padawan to even having a Mandolarion warrior.

This show puts an interesting twist on the Star Wars name by having the main group of characters as a bunch of unknown individuals who all had some connection to the fall of the Galactic Republic. I appreciate how this series can tie together a lot of concepts from the Republic Era to the Rebellion Era, while also mending some frustrating plot holes. This series also does an amazing job in bringing back older characters that have a purpose to this new story, rather than being used for pure nostalgia.
